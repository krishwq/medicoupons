NEET PG 2026: Complete Guide on Exam Dates, Syllabus, Pattern & Preparation Strategy

Published on March 6, 2026

The National Board of Examinations in Medical Sciences (NBEMS) has officially announced the exam date for the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test for Postgraduate (NEET PG) 2026. Scheduled for August 30, 2026, this highly competitive exam is the gateway to MD, MS, and PG Diploma courses across India.

Whether you are just starting your preparation or looking to streamline your study plan, this comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about NEET PG 2026 - from crucial dates and registration details to a powerful preparation strategy.

NEET PG 2026 Important Dates

Event Date

Online Registration Starts March - April 2026
Last Date to Apply Late April / May 2026
Admit Card Release Mid-August 2026
NEET PG 2026 Exam Date August 30, 2026 (Sunday)
Result Declaration September 2026
Internship Cut-off Date July - September 2026

NEET PG 2026 Exam Pattern

  • Mode of Exam: Online (CBT)
  • Duration: 3 hours 30 minutes (210 minutes)
  • Total Questions: 200 MCQs
  • Maximum Marks: 800
  • Language: English only
  • Marking Scheme: +4 for correct answer
  • Negative Marking: -1 for incorrect answer
  • Unattempted: 0 marks

How to Register for NEET PG 2026

  • New User Registration: Create User ID and Password with name, DOB, email and phone number.
  • Fill Application Form: Add education details, internship status and NMC/SMC registration.
  • Upload Documents: Upload photo, signature and thumb impression.
  • Select Exam City: Choose preferred exam center city.
  • Pay Fee: ₹3500 for General/OBC/EWS and ₹2500 for SC/ST/PwD.

How to Prepare for NEET PG 2026: A Winning Strategy

1. Build a Rock-Solid Foundation

Start with high-yield pre-clinical and para-clinical subjects. For subjects like Physiology, reading standard textbooks like Guyton and Hall or Ganong helps build strong concepts required for clinical questions.

2. Leverage Premium Digital Resources

Platforms like PrepLadder provide structured video lectures, curated QBank and high-yield notes designed specifically for the NEET PG pattern.

3. Maintain a Mistake Ledger

Because of negative marking, accuracy is critical. Maintain a notebook where you record mistakes from mock tests and revise them daily.

4. Spaced Repetition and Grand Tests

Finish the first revision by March 2026. Reserve the remaining months for revision and Grand Tests. Attempt one GT every two weeks initially and weekly near the exam.
